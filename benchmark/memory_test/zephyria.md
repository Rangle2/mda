# Zephyria — The Floating Continent

Zephyria is a continent that floats 3,000 meters above the ocean surface. It is held aloft by crystallized aether deposits embedded in its rock foundation. The continent has a surface area of 840,000 square kilometers and a population of 12 million.

## The Aether Crystals

Aether crystals form over 400 years inside deep bedrock under extreme pressure. They emit a low-frequency resonance at 7.3 hertz that repels gravitational pull. A single crystal of standard size weighs 14 kilograms and can sustain 200 tons of lift. The largest known crystal is called the Heartstone and is located beneath the capital city Verenhal. It weighs 9,400 kilograms and has been sustaining lift for 1,200 years.

## Verenhal — The Capital

Verenhal is built directly above the Heartstone deposit. The city has a population of 2.1 million. The city is governed by the Council of Arcs, which consists of seven elected members each serving a six-year term. The current head of the Council is Magistra Solenne Var, who has held the position for eleven years. Verenhal is known for its Spire District, where towers are built downward from the continent's underside and hang freely in open air.

## The Groundless Sea

The ocean below Zephyria is called the Groundless Sea. It receives no direct sunlight due to the continent's shadow and has developed a unique ecosystem based on bioluminescent organisms. The Groundless Sea reaches a depth of 14,000 meters at its deepest point, known as the Veil Trench. No human expedition has returned from below 6,000 meters.

## The Drift Miners

Drift Miners are workers who descend below the continent's edge to extract aether crystals from exposed rock faces. The profession has a mortality rate of 23 percent per year. Drift Miners are paid in Shards, the local currency, at a rate of 850 Shards per crystal extracted. The Drift Miners Guild was founded 340 years ago and currently has 4,200 registered members.

## The Collapse of Sector Nine

Forty years ago, a section of Zephyria known as Sector Nine lost aether resonance and fell into the Groundless Sea. The event killed 80,000 people. The cause was later identified as resonance interference caused by a Drift Miner drilling operation that fractured a subsurface crystal cluster. Following the collapse, the Council of Arcs banned all drilling within 500 meters of known crystal clusters and established the Resonance Monitoring Authority.

## The Resonance Monitoring Authority

The Resonance Monitoring Authority, also called the RMA, was established 39 years ago following the Sector Nine collapse. It operates 2,400 sensors distributed across the continent's underside. Each sensor measures aether resonance frequency every six seconds. When frequency drops below 6.8 hertz in any zone, the RMA issues a Drift Warning. Three consecutive Drift Warnings trigger mandatory evacuation of the affected zone.

## Solenne Var

Solenne Var became Magistra after her predecessor Aldric Tomb resigned following a corruption scandal involving falsified RMA sensor data. Var's first act as Magistra was to triple the RMA's budget. She is the daughter of a Drift Miner who died in Sector Nine. She has proposed a law that would make tampering with RMA data a capital offense but the Council has not yet passed it.

## The Spire District

The Spire District in Verenhal contains 34 hanging towers. The tallest is called the Needle and extends 600 meters below the continent's underside. The Needle houses the Zephyria Academy of Aether Sciences. The Academy trains 1,200 students per year and has produced every chief scientist in RMA history. The current head of the Academy is Professor Davin Orell, who previously worked as a Drift Miner for seven years before studying aether science.

## Aether Resonance Interference

Two aether crystal deposits that are closer than 80 meters to each other can cause resonance interference. Interference amplifies both frequencies and can cause crystal fracture if sustained for more than 72 hours. Crystal fracture eliminates lift capacity permanently. The Sector Nine collapse involved a cluster where two deposits were 31 meters apart, well within the interference range.

## The Shards Currency

Shards are minted from fragments of non-functional aether crystals that have lost their resonance. One Shard is approximately 2 grams of material. The exchange rate is fixed by the Council of Arcs at 1,000 Shards per unit of standard lift capacity per year. Drift Miners earn 850 Shards per extracted crystal, which represents roughly 85 percent of the crystal's official lift value.

## Davin Orell

Davin Orell spent seven years as a Drift Miner before leaving the profession after losing two colleagues in the same accident. He studied aether science at the Academy under Professor Lira Vas, who was the first scientist to mathematically model resonance interference. Orell's own research extended Vas's model to predict fracture timelines from interference duration. His fracture timeline model is now used by the RMA as the basis for its Drift Warning threshold calculations.
