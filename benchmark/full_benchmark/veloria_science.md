# Veloria — Science and Infrastructure

## Glowfield Panel Biology

Glowfield panels are cultivated from a fungal species called Lumiceps veloriana, found only in the geothermal zones of the First Tier. The species produces light through a bioluminescent reaction involving a compound called velorin. Velorin production peaks at temperatures between 28 and 36 degrees Celsius, which is why Glowfields only function in the Second Tier's controlled environment and not in the cooler Third Tier. The panels emit light at a wavelength of 520 nanometers, which falls in the green spectrum and is sufficient for photosynthesis of all 34 crop species grown in the Second Tier.

## The Spore-Resistant Coating

Toven Ral's spore-resistant coating is applied to the outer membrane of each Glowfield panel before installation. The coating is composed of three compounds: silicate binder, copper sulfate at 2 percent concentration, and a proprietary enzyme inhibitor designated RI-7. The RI-7 compound specifically disrupts the reproductive cycle of Ashfen fungal spores without affecting Lumiceps veloriana. The coating must be reapplied every six months. Failure to reapply on schedule increases spore vulnerability by 400 percent within 30 days.

## Kael Morn's Extension Research

Morn's lifespan extension method works by adding a nitrogen-fixing bacterial layer between the panel substrate and the Lumiceps veloriana culture. The bacteria, designated NF-3, convert ambient nitrogen into ammonia that the fungus uses as a nutrient supplement. This reduces the fungus's dependence on externally supplied nutrients by 60 percent, slowing the degradation that causes panels to fail at 18 months. Morn's lab trials showed a consistent extension to 25 months across 4,000 test panels. The pilot program uses 10,000 panels in grid sector G-7 of the southern Second Tier.

## Geothermal Infrastructure

The First Tier's geothermal vents provide both heat and electricity. Veloria operates 340 geothermal generators producing a combined output of 4,200 megawatts. This powers all three Tiers including the Glowfield network, the Vent pressure seals, and the tunnel transit system. The geothermal output has been stable for 200 years. The engineering team responsible for maintaining the generators is called the Deep Engineers, a guild of 1,400 specialists. The chief Deep Engineer is a woman named Sura Vass, who has held the position for 7 years.

## The Tunnel Transit System

Veloria's three Tiers are connected by a network of 88 transit tunnels. The tunnels use magnetic rail cars that travel at 140 kilometers per hour. The system carries 3.2 million passengers per day. The deepest tunnel connects Draloch directly to the transit hub at Vent Seven — the same vent used in the Grey Incursion. This tunnel is 2,100 meters long and takes 54 seconds to traverse at full speed. Following the Grey Incursion, this tunnel was equipped with three blast doors that can be sealed independently.

## The Ironblack Mines

The Ironblack mines occupy the lowest accessible zones of the First Tier, at depths between 2,600 and 3,100 meters. Mining is performed by 6,200 workers, of whom 4,100 are convicted criminals serving labor sentences. The mines produce 8,400 kilograms of Ironblack per month. The Obsidian Court takes 70 percent of all Ironblack as a state tax, leaving 30 percent for licensed private refiners. The current overseer of the Ironblack mines is a Court-appointed official named Drev Holst, who has held the position for 4 years.
