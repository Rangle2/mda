# Veloria — Economy and Key Figures

## The Glowfield Economy

Veloria's economy depends entirely on the Glowfield network. Without artificial light, Second Tier agriculture collapses. The total Glowfield network consists of 2.2 million panels across the Second Tier. Replacing all panels on the standard 18-month cycle requires 122,000 replacements per month. Each panel costs 40 Depths — the Velorian currency — to produce. The monthly replacement cost for the entire network is 4,880,000 Depths.

## The Depths Currency

Depths are minted from a dense mineral called Ironblack found only in the First Tier's geothermal zones. One Depth coin weighs 8 grams. The Obsidian Court controls all Ironblack mining and minting. The exchange rate with surface traders is fixed at 3 Depths per surface gold coin. Veloria exports processed minerals and fungal medicines in exchange for surface goods including timber, textiles, and metals that cannot be produced underground.

## Toven Ral

Toven Ral was born in the Second Tier to a Glowfield maintenance family. He spent twelve years as a field mycologist before developing the spore-resistant coating that ended the Ashfen Sickness. He refused the Warden-Prime's offer of a noble title, preferring to establish the VIFS instead. He trained 14 students who went on to senior positions in Veloria's fungal science community, including Kael Morn. Ral died 12 years ago at age 71 from complications related to prolonged exposure to the geothermal zones during his research.

## Kael Morn

Kael Morn grew up in Draloch in the First Tier. He was accepted to the VIFS at age 16, making him the youngest student ever admitted. He studied directly under Toven Ral for nine years. His doctoral research demonstrated that adding a secondary nitrogen-fixing layer to the fungal substrate could extend panel lifespan by 40 percent. The Obsidian Court has funded a pilot program to test Morn's method on 10,000 panels in the southern Second Tier. If successful, the program would reduce monthly replacement costs by 2,000,000 Depths annually.

## Isara Denn

Isara Denn became Warden-Prime after her father Aldric Denn died in office. She was 31 at the time of her appointment, making her the youngest Warden-Prime in Veloria's history. Her first act was to commission the pressure seals for all 14 Vents following the Grey Incursion, which occurred three months before she took office. She is known for prioritizing infrastructure over military spending, which has drawn criticism from the garrison commanders who argue that the 200-soldier requirement per Vent is insufficient. Isara Denn personally funded Kael Morn's pilot program from the Warden-Prime's discretionary budget.

## The Grey Incursion

The Grey Incursion occurred 15 years ago when a group of 340 surface raiders entered through Vent Seven using stolen Vent Authority credentials. They descended to the Second Tier before a garrison force intercepted them. The battle lasted six hours and killed 47 Velorian soldiers and 210 raiders. The remaining 130 raiders were captured and sentenced to labor in the Ironblack mines. The credentials used in the incursion were traced to a corrupt Vent Authority official named Prest Gallen, who was executed three months later. Following the incursion, Isara Denn ordered the pressure seals and mandatory garrisons.
